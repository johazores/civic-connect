## One-liner

A mobile-first citizen reporting and service tracking platform for cities, barangays, and community organizations.

## Short pitch

Civic Connect helps local organizations collect citizen concerns, assign them to the right team, and keep residents updated with a simple tracking number.

## Longer pitch

Civic Connect is a white-label civic service platform for local governments and community teams. Citizens can submit issues, upload photos, track progress, view services, read announcements, and access hotlines from a mobile-friendly public app. Staff can manage reports, assign departments, publish updates, and maintain public content from one admin dashboard.

## Main value propositions

### For citizens

- Easy mobile reporting
- Reference number for every concern
- Clear status tracking
- Public updates without repeated follow-ups
- Services, news, and hotlines in one place

### For staff

- Centralized report queue
- Department assignment
- Status and priority management
- Public/internal update trail
- CSV export
- Easy public content management

### For leadership

- More transparent request handling
- Better visibility into common concerns
- Cleaner reporting process
- Branded public service experience
- Faster pilot than building from scratch

## Ideal first customers

Start with customers who need the workflow but have less procurement friction:

- Barangays
- Subdivision homeowners associations
- Campus facility teams
- Churches or community organizations
- Local property/facility teams
- Small municipalities

## Suggested pricing ideas

These are starter ideas only and should be adjusted after pilot feedback.

### Setup + monthly

- Setup: ₱15,000 to ₱50,000
- Monthly: ₱3,000 to ₱15,000

Good for small organizations that want low commitment.

### Annual package

- ₱60,000 to ₱180,000 per year depending on support, storage, and customization.

Good for organizations with yearly budgets.

### Custom city package

- Custom quote for larger tenants needing custom domain, SMS, audit logs, integrations, or formal reports.

## Objections and answers

### “We already use Facebook Messenger.”

Messenger is useful for conversation, but it does not provide structured categories, department assignment, status tracking, export, or a clean request history.

### “Will this replace our official systems?”

No. It can start as a lightweight intake and tracking layer. Integrations can be added later if needed.

### “Can citizens still report without an account?”

Yes. Guest reporting is supported. Registered citizens get an easier dashboard.

### “Can each city have its own branding?”

Yes. Each tenant has its own name, city name, tagline, description, contact details, and primary color.

## Best next marketing step
